# Session Rules — When You Trade

All times in **Pacific Time** unless noted.

## Primary Window — 6:30 AM to 10:00 AM PT

This is the NY open through ~1 PM ET. Roughly 3.5 hours. Most of your edge lives here.

Inside this window, two especially high-value sub-windows:

- **6:30 - 7:30 AM PT (NY open volatility):** First hour of cash session. Volatility expansion, fresh order flow, levels get tested aggressively. SETUP-1 (sweep + FVG continuation) prints frequently.
- **9:30 - 10:00 AM PT (pre-lunch positioning):** Last push before NY lunch chop. Often a clean trend continuation or a sharp reversal at level. Either setup works here.

## Occasional Windows

- **3:00 PM PT (US after-hours futures, ES/NQ):** Lower volume, but levels can break clean post-cash-session. Trade only if you have a strong HTF read.
- **5:00 PM PT (Asia open):** GC and SI especially. Asian session has its own character — slow grinds with sharp wicks. Be patient with entries.

## Avoid

- **12:30 - 1:30 PM PT (NY lunch chop):** Liquidity drops, levels get faked, FVGs print and immediately fail. No setups in this window unless news drops.
- **Outside the windows above:** No trades. Even if a setup looks perfect.

## News Days

If a high-impact event is scheduled (FOMC, CPI, NFP, presidential speech) inside your trading window:

- 30 minutes pre-event: stop entering new trades. Manage existing.
- Event candle: do not trade the spike. Mark the wick as a relevant level for later.
- 15-30 minutes post-event: wait for the dust to settle. A clean SETUP-1 off the news wick is one of the highest-probability setups available — but only after the volatility compresses.

## Session Handoff

End of session ≠ end of trading career. When you leave the window, leave. Don't paper-trade the chop "to keep my eye in." That's how you tilt yourself into bad habits.
