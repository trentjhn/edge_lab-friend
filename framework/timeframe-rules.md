# Timeframe Rules — Day Trading

## TF Hierarchy

- **4H / 1H — HTF context.** Determines bias. All entries must align. If multiple 1H candles confirm the same read, you can rely on the 1H alone; otherwise use 4H as the tiebreaker.
- **15m / 5m — Mid layer.** Where the setup forms. The sweep + FVG (SETUP-1) or the iFVG retest (SETUP-2) is visible here. If multiple 5m bars confirm the structure, fold up to 15m to confirm.
- **1m — Execution layer.** Entry timing only. Never generates a thesis. Never overrides higher TF.

## Hard Rules

1. Establish 4H or 1H bias before looking at any 5m setup.
2. If 4H and 1H conflict → no trade. Wait.
3. A 5m setup fighting the 1H trend is not a setup.
4. Use 15m to confirm the 5m setup — not to generate a different thesis.
5. 1m noise is not a signal. A 1m reversal candle without 5m support = noise.
6. Do not use 1m moves to justify exiting early when the 5m says hold.

## Holding Periods

- **Setups forming on 5m → typical hold ≤ 5 minutes, up to 10 in volatile windows.**
- **Setups forming on 15m → can extend to 15 minutes, occasionally up to 30.**
- **Manage by 1m close at execution; manage by 5m structure during the trade.**

## Entry Timing

- Wait for the trigger candle to close on 1m. Do not anticipate.
- 1m can be used for entry refinement within the 5m FVG zone.
- If the 1m close misses the FVG, the entry is missed — wait for the next setup.
- Do not chase. A missed entry is better than a bad entry.

## Decision Timing

- Plan the trade on 1H/4H + 5m/15m before entry.
- Once in, only the original stop and target matter unless 5m structure shifts (MSS or BOS against you).
- Do not re-plan mid-trade.
